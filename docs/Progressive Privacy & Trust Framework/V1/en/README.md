## 1. Why AryaChain Chose Progressive Privacy

Most blockchains exist at one of two extremes:  
- Total transparency (like Bitcoin or Ethereum), or  
- Complete opacity (like Monero or Zcash).

AryaChain takes a middle path ‚Äî one that embraces human choice.  
Instead of forcing everyone to live in full light or full shadow, users decide how private each account should be.  
We call this idea **Progressive Privacy** ‚Äî a model where privacy grows with your trust and participation, not with your isolation.

This approach builds a bridge between:
- individual privacy,

## 2. The Three Layers of Privacy

| Level | Hidden Elements | Visible Elements | Purpose |
|:------|:----------------|:----------------|:---------|
| **Level 0 ‚Äî Transparent** | Nothing | Everything | Fully open and auditable, similar to Bitcoin. |
| **Level 1 ‚Äî Confidential** | Transaction amount | Sender and receiver addresses | Semi-private: hides value but keeps traceability. |
| **Level 2 ‚Äî Deep Private** | Amount and addresses | Encrypted or stealth | Maximum confidentiality for users or institutions requiring full privacy. |

Each account defines its privacy level at creation.  
And **each transaction inherits the level of its sender** ‚Äî never higher, never lower.

This simple rule keeps privacy voluntary, logical, and consistent.

## 3. Trust Points ‚Äî The Currency of Integrity

Instead of giving power to wealth or hardware, AryaChain rewards **constructive behavior**.  
Each node earns a **Trust Point (TP)** score based on its history and contribution to the network.

- Nodes with **low TP** can validate blocks and observe public data, but they cannot access confidential state or verify encrypted data.  
- Nodes with **high TP** can decrypt metadata (as allowed by policy), verify confidential proofs, and update private account states.  
- Voting power and governance weight scale with TP.

This model transforms trust into a measurable resource ‚Äî something you earn through positive participation, not privilege.

## 5. Balancing Privacy, Trust, and Law

True decentralization requires responsibility, not invisibility.  
AryaChain introduces a **Disclosure Policy** for confidential transactions ‚Äî

Combined with the **Emergency Disclosure Key (EDK)** system, this allows controlled, lawful access in extreme cases ‚Äî  
without centralized authority or unrestricted exposure.

Privacy remains the default.  
Disclosure becomes the exception ‚Äî traceable, multi-party, and transparent.
